Long-Board Dancing

Often viewed as an artsy subset of freestyling, dancing involves walking and cross stepping on a moving longboard, performing elegant and stylish body spinning movements and even actual dancing, all while keeping the board rolling and carving on flat surface. Learning to dance on a longboard requires patience and skills, but results in a beautiful riding style where agility, balance, coordination, and grace of motion all combine.Dancing is a resurgence of old-school tricks in longboarding that involves a variety of walking and spinning moves.

Dancing originates from boardwalking in surfings. A board is usually considered a dancing board when it is 45 in (1,100 mm), or more, long and can be up to 12 in (300 mm) wide, and although most of the tricks can be performed on smaller decks, a larger deck provides a more comfortable platform and is less restrictive to maneuver.
